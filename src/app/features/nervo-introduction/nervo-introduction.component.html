Human nervous system

Introduction

The Nervous System is the control center for your body. It interprets the things your body senses, and it sends information to the muscles and glands, telling them what to do. It also runs the systems you donâ€™t have to think about, like the digestive and cardiovascular systems. The nervous system is also responsible for your moods and your thoughts. The structures of the nervous system include your brain, spinal cord, and nerves that reach to every part of your body. The human nervous system can be viewed in two parts, which include the CNS (central nervous system) and the PNS (peripheral nervous system). The brain and the spinal cord comprise the CNS, whereas the PNS connects the rest of the body, such as vital organs to the spinal cord and the brain.
